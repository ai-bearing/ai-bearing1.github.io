If I'm being honest, I wasn't particularly adept at high school (or rather, I don't think I was uniquely adept at math compared to other subjects) — my AMC scores in high school were, for lack of a better word, extremely bad, and while I had good grades, I was mostly coasting off YouTube videos and various other snippets of knowledge I had accrued over the years. In fact, I didn't even take a formal math class my senior year of high school, and I was seriously considering doing a physics major.

I had a lot of anxiety going into college surrounding doing a math major — my self esteem was pretty low thanks to the high school I went to having been a very competitive outfit with lots of genuinely bright people, and I thought I would not be able to excel in my math classes to the point of doing a math major.
